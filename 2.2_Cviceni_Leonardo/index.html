Leonardo da Vinci

Introduction

Leonardo da Vinci was an Italian polymath of the Renaissance period, known for his contributions to various fields, including art, science, and engineering.

Early Life

Leonardo da Vinci was born on April 15, 1452, in Vinci, Italy, as the illegitimate son of a notary and a peasant woman.

Artistic Masterpieces

He is famous for creating some of the world's most iconic artworks, including the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper.

Scientific Inventions

Leonardo also designed and conceptualized numerous inventions, such as the flying machine and the armored tank.

Legacy

His work and ideas continue to influence and inspire artists, scientists, and innovators to this day.

Selected Works

  1. Mona Lisa
  2. The Last Supper
  3. Vitruvian Man

Family

  • Parents: Ser Piero da Vinci (father) and Caterina (mother)
